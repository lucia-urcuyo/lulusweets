## Ingredients

| Ingredients | 24 |
| :---- | ----- |
| Bread Flour | 500 |
| Salt | 10 |
| Active Dry Yeast | 10 |
| Granulated Sugar | 60 |
| Eggs | (4) |
| Water | 125 |
| Butter | 125 |

## Instructions

1. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the dough hook, combine the flour, salt, yeast, and sugar. Mix briefly to combine.

2. Add the eggs and mix on low while adding the water in a slow and steady stream.

3. Increase the mixer speed to medium and mix for another 8 minutes.

4. While the mixer is on low speed, add the butter a tablespoon at a time.

5. Mix on medium speed for 5 minutes.

6. Reduce the mixer speed to low, and add the butter a little at a time, waiting until it is fully incorporated into the dough before adding the next piece.

7. Once the butter is fully incorporated, increase the mixer speed to medium, and mix for a further 5 minutes, until the dough is very soft and smooth.

8. Transfer to an oiled bowl and cover with plastic wrap. Place in a warm spot until doubled in size, approximately 1 ½ – 2 hours. Alternatively, the first rise can be done overnight in the fridge.

9. Punch the dough to release the air and transfer it onto a well-floured surface and finish pushing all of the air out.

10. Weigh the dough and divide into 24 equal weight portions. Roll each portion into a tight ball. Place the rolled doughnuts on a floured baking tray, leaving adequate space between and cover with a greased plastic wrap.

11. Leave the doughnuts to proof for an hour.

12. While the doughnuts are proofing, heat the neutral oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot to 310°f / 155°c. Place a wire rack over a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Place the sugar in a bowl.

13. Once the oil has come to temperature, gently lower the doughnuts, two at a time, into the hot oil. Cook for 1½-2 minutes each side, until golden brown.
14. Remove from the oil using a slotted spoon and place on a cooling rack. Allow to cool for 30 seconds before tossing in the sugar.
15. Once the doughnuts are totally cool, poke a hole till the middle of the donut using a small knife and wiggle it a little bit to widen the hole.
16. Transfer the filling to a piping bag filled with a round piping tip. Fill each doughnut, you will feel it become heavy in your hand, until it begins to come out the top.
