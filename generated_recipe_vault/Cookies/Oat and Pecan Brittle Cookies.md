## Ingredients

| Pecan Brittle |  |
| :---- | :---: |
| Pecans | 142 |
| Granulated Sugar | 150 |
| Unsalted Butter | 56.7 |
| Baking Soda | 3.5 |
| Salt | 3 |

| Cookies |  |
| :---- | :---: |
| Unsalted Butter | 230 |
| All Purpose Flour | 173 |
| Salt | 12 |
| Baking Soda | 7 |
| Old Fashioned Oats | 200 |
| Dark Brown Sugar | 150 |
| Granulated Sugar | 100 |
| Eggs | (2) |
| Vanilla Extract | 4.2 |

## Instructions

### For the Pecan Brittle

1. Preheat oven to 350°.
2. Toast pecans on a baking sheet, tossing halfway through, until slightly darkened and fragrant, 8–10 minutes. Let cool.
3. Line another baking sheet with a Silpat baking mat.
4. Cook granulated sugar, butter, and 2 Tbsp. water in a small saucepan over medium-low heat, stirring gently with a heatproof rubber spatula, until sugar is dissolved.
5. Increase heat to medium and bring syrup to a rapid simmer.
6. Cook, without stirring, swirling pan often, until syrup turns a deep amber color, 8–10 minutes.
7. Immediately remove saucepan from heat and stir in pecans.
8. Once pecans are well coated, add baking soda and salt and stir to incorporate (mixture will foam and sputter as baking soda aerates caramel).
9. Working quickly (it will harden fast), scrape mixture onto prepared baking sheet and spread into a thin layer.
10. Let cool completely, 5–10 minutes. Chop into pea-size pieces; set aside.

### For the Cookies

1. Place half of butter in the bowl of a stand mixer.
2. Bring remaining butter to a boil in a small saucepan over medium-low heat, stirring often with a heatproof rubber spatula.
3. Cook, scraping bottom and sides of pan constantly, until butter sputters, foams, and, eventually, you see browned bits floating on the surface, 5–7 minutes.
4. Pour brown butter over butter in stand mixer bowl, making sure to scrape in all the browned bits. Let sit until butter begins to resolidify, about 30 minutes.
5. Pulse flour, salt, and baking soda in a food processor to combine.
6. Add half of reserved pecan brittle and 100 grams of oats; process in long pulses until oats and brittle are finely ground.
7. Add brown sugar and granulated sugar to butter and beat with paddle attachment on medium speed until light and smooth but not fluffy, about 2 minutes.
8. Scrape down sides of bowl and add eggs and vanilla. Beat until very light and satiny, about 1 minute.
9. Scrape down sides of bowl and add flour mixture; beat on low speed until no dry spots remain and you have a soft, evenly mixed dough.
10. Add remaining half of brittle and remaining 1 cup oats; mix on low speed just to distribute.
11. Scoop level portions of 1.3 oz to make 27-28 cookies.
12. Place on a parchment-lined baking sheet, spacing as close together as possible. Cover tightly with plastic wrap and chill at least 12 hours and up to 2 days.
13. When ready to bake, preheat to 350°. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper. Arrange 6 cookies on each prepared baking sheet, spacing at least 3" apart.
14. Bake cookies, rotating baking sheets top to bottom and front to back after 12 minutes, until dark golden brown around the edges, 16–20 minutes.
15. Let cookies cool 5 minutes on baking sheets, then transfer cookies to a wire rack with a spatula and let cool completely.
