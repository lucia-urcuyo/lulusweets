## Ingredients

| Ingredients |  |
| :---- | :---: |
| Egg White Powder | 4 |
| Egg Whites | 100 |
| Granulated Sugar | 100 |
| Almond Flour | 105 |
| Powdered Sugar | 105 |

## Instructions

1. Pre-heat the oven to295ºF.
2. Prepare a large piping bag fitted with a large round tip and line two baking sheets with a silicone mat.
3. Sift the powdered sugar and almond flour together. Set it aside.
4. Whisk the sugar and the egg white powder in a bowl and place it over a double boiler with barely simmering water. Make sure the bottom of the bowl isn’t touching the simmering water.
5. Whisk the mixture until frothy and the sugar is completely melted without overheating the sugar syrup.
6. Transfer the syrup to the bowl of a stand mixer.
7. With the whisk attachment, start whisking the mixture on low for about 30 seconds, then gradually start increasing the speed to medium. Whisk on medium for one to two minutes, until the mixture is white and starting to become fluffy. Raise the speed to medium-high and whisk for a few minutes until stiff peaks are formed. When you pull your whip up, the peak should be stiff and shooting straight up, with possibly a slight bend at the top, but not bending down to the side.
8. Pour the sifted powdered sugar and almond flour into the stiff meringue.
9. Start folding gently with a spatula.
10. Add food coloring, if using.
11. Stop folding when the batter is glossy and has a thick and flowing consistency. To test it, pick up some batter with the spatula and try to draw a figure 8 with the batter that is dripping off the spatula. If you can form several 8 figures without the batter breaking up, that’s one indication that it might be ready. Another way to test it is to grab a teaspoon of batter and spoon it onto the parchment paper or silicon mat. Wait a minute to see how it behaves. If the batter stays stiff, forming a point, and doesn’t spread out, fold a little bit more. If the batter spreads out a bit and starts to look glossy and smooth on top, it’s ready.
12. Transfer the batter to the piping bag.
13. Place the piping bag directly 90 degrees over the center of each macaron template. Apply gentle pressure and carefully pipe for about 3 seconds, and then quickly pull the bag up twisting slightly.
14. Bang the trays against the counter a few times each.
15. Use a toothpick to pop any air bubbles in the surface of the shells.
16. Let the trays sit for 20-40 minutes until the macaron shells are dry.
17. Bake one tray at a time for 13 minutes, rotating every 5 minutes.
